Milk Fever (Goat)

Symptoms

  • Lethargy
  • Poor appetite
  • Poor milk production
  • Coma
  • Agitation
  • Muscle tremors
  • Inability to rise

Cure

  • Milk Fever - Cures

    Preventive Measures

    • Limit calcium intake
    • Feed low potassium diets
    • Use anionic salts
    • Provide calcium supplements: Within a few hours after calving, provide cows with an oral calcium supplement, such as a bolus or paste.
    • Monitor urine pH: The target urine pH is between 5.5 and 6.0.
    • Supplement with magnesium: Supplement magnesium at the rate of 15 to 20 grams per day along with a source of easily digestible carbohydrate.
    • Shorten the dry period
    • Prepartum milking
    • Reduce milking in early lactation

    Chemical Measures

    • Milk fever cases should be treated with 500 milliliters of 23 percent calcium gluconate IV and followed by the administration of two oral calcium boluses given 12 hours apart.

    Biological Measures

    • NA

    Indigenous Methods

    • Provision of germinated gram and green fodder
