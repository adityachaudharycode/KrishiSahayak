Plague

Symptoms

  • Respiratory distress
  • Decreased appetite
  • Decreased egg production
  • Swelling
  • Blood-tinged discharge
  • Cyanosis
  • Pin-point hemorrhages
  • Incoordination
  • Dark red/white spots

Cure

  • Plague - Cures

    Preventive Measures

    • Vaccination: Vaccinate poultry with the Pasteurella multocida vaccine to prevent the spread of the bacteria causing the plague.
    • Quarantine: Isolate newly introduced birds to prevent contamination with infected birds.
    • Biosecurity: Practice strict biosecurity protocols â€“ disinfect equipment, avoid contact with wild birds or rodents, and control the movement of people and animals.
    • Proper Sanitation: Regularly clean and disinfect poultry houses, equipment, and feeders to reduce the spread of infection.
    • Rodent Control: Implement rodent control measures as rodents can carry the bacteria responsible for the plague.

    Chemical Measures

    • Antibiotics: Administer antibiotics like Streptomycin or Tetracycline to treat infected poultry under veterinary guidance.
    • Disinfectants: Use strong disinfectants like phenol or formaldehyde to sterilize poultry houses, equipment, and waterers.
    • Antiseptic Treatments: Topical antiseptics, such as iodine solution, can be applied to wounds or infections in affected birds.

    Bio-Chemical Methods

    • Probiotics: Add probiotics to poultry feed to support the immune system and restore gut health after an infection.
    • Herbal Remedies: Use Tulsi (Holy Basil) or Ginger in feed to help boost immunity and fight bacterial infections.
    • Garlic: Incorporate garlic into poultry feed to help combat infections due to its antibacterial properties.
    • Vitamin Supplements: Provide Vitamin C and Vitamin E to enhance the immune system and improve recovery.

    Indigenous Methods

    • Neem Leaves: Boil Neem leaves in water and provide it to poultry as a natural antibacterial and immune booster.
    • Turmeric & Ginger: Mix turmeric and ginger powder in water or feed to reduce inflammation and improve overall health.
    • Ash and Garlic: A mixture of wood ash and crushed garlic can be used in the chicken coop to help disinfect the environment and control bacterial growth.
    • Cumin and Fenugreek: Boil cumin and fenugreek seeds and give the water to the birds to improve their immunity.
