Enterotoxemia

Symptoms

  • Sudden onset
  • Diarrhea
  • Abdominal pain
  • Neurologic signs
  • Lethargy
  • Sudden death

Cure

  • Enterotoxaemia - Cures

    Preventive Measures

    • Vaccinate with Clostridial vaccines (7-way or 8-way vaccines).
    • Avoid sudden diet changes (especially high grain/sugary feed).
    • Maintain good hygiene: Keep housing clean, dry, and well-ventilated.
    • Reduce stress: Avoid stressful handling, transport, or environmental changes.

    Chemical Measures

    • Antibiotics: Use penicillin or other antibiotics to control bacterial growth.
    • Anti-toxins: Administer Clostridium perfringens antitoxin early to neutralize toxins.
    • Prophylactic Drugs: Use antibiotics like penicillin in high-risk animals.

    Bio-Chemical Methods

    • Probiotics/Prebiotics: Use probiotics (e.g., Lactobacillus) to support gut health.
    • Organic Acids: Add formic or acetic acid to feed to reduce harmful bacteria.
    • Yeast-based Feed Additives: Use yeast supplements to stabilize gut microbiota.

    Indian Jugaad Methods

    • Amla Powder: Mix Amla (Indian Gooseberry) powder with water to boost immunity.
    • Turmeric & Ginger: Mix turmeric and ginger powder to improve gut health.
    • Neem Leaves: Boil Neem leaves and give the extract to cleanse the digestive system.
    • Salt & Baking Soda: Add salt and baking soda in water to regulate stomach pH.
    • Ash & Garlic: Mix wood ash with crushed garlic to aid digestion and treat bacterial infections.
