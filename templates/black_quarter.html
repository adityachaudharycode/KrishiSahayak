Black Quarter (BQ)

Symptoms

  • Sudden high fever (107ºF-108ºF)
  • Stops eating and ruminating
  • Hot and painful swelling on the loin and buttocks
  • Lameness
  • Crackling sound when pressed
  • Animals usually die within 24-48 hours of symptoms appearing

Cure

  • BQ (Black Quarter)

    Preventive Measures

    • Vaccination with BQ vaccine at 4-6 months and annual vaccination
    • Isolation and disinfection of infected areas
    • Proper disposal of carcass

    Chemical Measures

    • Antibiotics like Penicillin, Tetracycline
    • Anti-inflammatory drugs

    Biological Measures

    • Provide proper nutrition to strengthen immunity

    Indigenous Method

    • Use turmeric and neem-based solutions for wound treatment
