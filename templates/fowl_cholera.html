Fowl Cholera

Symptoms

  • Fever
  • Loss of appetite
  • Ruffled feathers
  • Mucous discharge from the mouth
  • Green watery diarrhea
  • Respiratory difficulty
  • Sudden deaths
  • Localized swelling in the joints, foot pads, eyes, and throat
  • Exudative conjunctivitis
  • Pharyngitis
  • Torticollis
  • Depression
  • Lack of coordination
  • Lameness
  • Increased mortality

Cure

  • Preventive Measures

    • Vaccination: Use Avian Influenza vaccines (H5N1, H7N9) in high-risk areas.
    • Biosecurity: Maintain strict biosecurity measures â€“ isolate new birds, control movement, and disinfect equipment regularly.
    • Limit contact with wild birds: Keep poultry away from wild birds that might carry the virus.
    • Quarantine: Quarantine new or potentially infected birds before mixing with the flock.
    • Control Feed & Water: Provide clean feed and water, and ensure proper sanitation.

    Chemical Measures

    • Antiviral Medications: Oseltamivir or Amantadine for treating infected birds (used under veterinary supervision).
    • Disinfectants: Use broad-spectrum disinfectants (e.g., iodine-based, formaldehyde) to clean equipment, coops, and facilities.
    • Sanitizing Footwear: Disinfect boots and equipment to avoid contamination spread between farms.

    Bio-Chemical Methods

    • Probiotics: Add probiotic supplements to poultry feed to boost immune system and gut health.
    • Herbal Remedies: Use Tulsi (Holy Basil) and Garlic in feed to enhance immune response and act as natural antivirals.
    • Vitamin C: Supplement with Vitamin C to strengthen immune defense against viral infections.

    Indigenous Methods

    • Neem Leaves: Boil Neem leaves in water and give the extract to birds as a natural disinfectant and immune booster.
    • Turmeric and Ginger: Add turmeric and ginger powder to feed to reduce inflammation and improve immunity.
    • Garlic and Lemon: Mix crushed garlic with lemon juice in water to improve resistance to infections.
    • Ash and Mustard Oil: Apply a mixture of wood ash and mustard oil to bird coop surfaces to repel viruses and bacteria.
